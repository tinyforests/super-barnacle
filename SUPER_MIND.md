# SUPER_MIND.md

The master internal document for Gardener & Son.

Read this before working on any Gardener & Son web property. It governs the strategic, ecological, visual, tonal, and systems logic of everything G&S builds. `AI_CONTEXT.md` is the quick-start companion to this file. Repo-specific engineering detail lives in each repo's `AGENTS.md` and `/docs/`.

This document is internal. It is not published. The public feels the Super Mind through clarity, beauty, restraint, ecology, coherence, and long-term care — never through over-explanation.

---

## 1. What Gardener & Son is

Gardener & Son is not a garden design studio that has added some side projects. It is a single piece of infrastructure with one purpose: **making private land legible as civic ecology.**

Melbourne's private gardens cover more land than all its parks combined. Most of that land does little ecological work — exotic planting, compacted soil, water sent straight to the gutter. Some of it, designed differently, does a great deal: corridors, habitat, water retention, indigenous restoration. Gardener & Son exists to design that second kind of garden, and then to measure it, credential it, connect it, and eventually pay for it.

G&S is, in one organism:

- An ecological garden design studio
- An indigenous micro-nursery (Plants of Place)
- An heirloom object house (Heirloom)
- A public storytelling platform
- A registry for ecological gardens (the Ecological Registry)
- A discovery layer for place-based plants and EVCs (Find My Ecological Garden, Find My EVC, Find My Native Plants)
- A future incentives and aggregation system (Yield, Curbing)
- A civic participant (Mont Albert Traders Association, council relationships)

These are not separate businesses. They are organs of one body.

## 2. The spine — Culture → Discovery → Registry → Incentives

Every G&S property sits at one of four stages. This is the single most important idea in this document. When working on any property, know which stage it serves and how it feeds the next.

**Culture.** The studio work, the gardens themselves, Heirloom, the storytelling, the Field Guide, the studios as ecological discovery centres. Culture is how people come to *want* an ecological garden — through beauty, story, and example. Culture creates desire and legitimacy.

**Discovery.** Find My Ecological Garden, Find My EVC, Find My Native Plants, Plants of Place. Discovery is how a person who now *wants* an ecological garden finds out what their place actually is — their EVC, their indigenous palette, what belongs. Discovery converts desire into knowledge.

**Registry.** The Ecological Registry. The Registry is how a garden that has been *made* becomes *legible* — measured, scored, verified, recorded, connected to the network. The Registry is the trust layer. It converts knowledge and work into a credentialed, permanent record.

**Incentives.** Yield, Curbing. The economic activation layer. Incentives convert verified ecological function into value — income for stewards, biodiversity infrastructure for councils, measurable outcomes for sponsors. Incentives convert the record into an economy.

The spine flows one way: Culture creates desire, Discovery creates knowledge, Registry creates trust, Incentives create value. Each stage feeds the next. A change to any property should strengthen its stage and respect the flow.

---

## 3. The honest sequence

G&S does not overpromise. The business is built in a deliberate order, and no public language may imply a later stage is active before it is.

1. **Design and installation.** The studio's core work. Active.
2. **Steward Review.** Paid ecological assessment that places a garden on the Registry. Active. The current commercial product of the Registry.
3. **Council pilots.** Paid council partnerships for measurement and reporting. Triggered when a council reaches enough registered gardens to make a pilot credible.
4. **Yield at scale.** Sponsor-funded annual income for stewards. Activated only when councils are active and the sponsor pipeline is real.

Skipping stages in language is the cardinal sin. Yield is not paying anyone today. The Registry is not a financial product yet. Say what is true now, and speak of what is coming in the future tense.

## 4. The operating Minds

When Gardener & Son makes a decision, it is never made from a single point of view. The studio thinks through a set of Minds — distinct lenses, each asking its own question. A good decision has been examined by every Mind that bears on it. The Super Mind is the coordinator: it does not replace the others, it holds them in balance.

These Minds are an internal tool. They are never published, never named on public surfaces, never turned into branding. They are how G&S thinks, not how G&S advertises.

### Gardener Mind

The Mind of the hands and the seasons. It asks: *will this actually grow? Is it true to how plants and soil and weather behave?* The Gardener Mind is suspicious of cleverness that ignores maintenance, climate, and time. It thinks in years, not launches. It is the Mind that keeps the work honest to living systems. When a plan is beautiful on paper but wrong for the site, the Gardener Mind is what says so.

### Designer Mind

The Mind of form, composition, and restraint. It asks: *is this beautiful, and is the beauty earned?* The Designer Mind owns proportion, material, light, typography, the line. It is the Mind behind the brand book's discipline — border-radius zero, the Signal Principle, Abril kept ceremonial. It distrusts decoration and prizes clarity. Beautiful but not decorative is its standard.

### Ecologist Mind

The Mind of function and evidence. It asks: *does this actually do ecological work, and can we prove it?* The Ecologist Mind owns EVCs, indigenous-versus-native precision, the scoring methodology, habitat function, the refusal to overclaim. It is conservative by temperament — it would rather a score be honest and low than flattering and false. It is the Mind that protects the Registry's credibility.

### Systems Mind

The Mind of the whole. It asks: *how does this connect — does it strengthen the organism or fragment it?* The Systems Mind sees the spine, the data flows, the way Discovery feeds the Registry feeds Yield. It owns architecture, schema, the connective tissue. It is the Mind that notices when a project is drifting into a silo, when two data files disagree, when a change helps one property and weakens another.

### Steward Mind

The Mind of the homeowner. It asks: *is this honest and meaningful to the person who lives with this garden?* The Steward Mind owns the warm voice, the idea of legacy and permanence, the framing of owners as stewards. It insists the work be invitational, never intimidating, never judgemental. It is the Mind that makes sure rigour does not curdle into coldness.

### Civic Mind

The Mind of the commons. It asks: *is this legible and useful to councils, planners, and the public good?* The Civic Mind owns the institutional voice, the council relationships, the data-sharing posture, the framing of private gardens as civic infrastructure. It thinks about the city, not just the client. It is the Mind that makes G&S a participant in the public realm, not just a private practice.

### Commercial Mind

The Mind of viability. It asks: *does this sustain the studio — honestly?* The Commercial Mind owns pricing, the revenue sequence, the funnel, the question of what pays for the work. It is not cynical; it is the Mind that knows beautiful work must be paid for to continue. Its discipline is the honest sequence — it refuses revenue that requires overclaiming.

### Object Mind

The Mind of things worth keeping. It asks: *does this object carry meaning, age well, and belong?* The Object Mind owns Heirloom — the curated vintage garden objects — and the tools, vessels, and cultural fragments connected to gardens. It thinks about permanence, patina, and the difference between a product and an heirloom.

### Storyteller Mind

The Mind of language and meaning. It asks: *does this carry the truth of the work in a way people feel?* The Storyteller Mind owns the Substack, the field notes, the garden stories, the Field Guide. It is the Mind that turns measurement into meaning without exaggeration — one literary moment per field note, restraint as the source of credibility.

### Super Mind

The coordinating Mind. It does not have its own narrow question; it holds all the others. It asks: *have the right Minds been consulted, and is the whole still coherent?* The Super Mind is what this document serves. When two Minds conflict — the Designer Mind wants restraint, the Commercial Mind wants conversion — the Super Mind is the judgement that resolves them in favour of the whole and the long term.

## 5. Voice and tone

Across all G&S properties the voice is:

- Calm
- Grounded
- Ecologically literate
- Beautiful but not decorative
- Intelligent but not corporate
- Human and humble
- Systems-aware

The voice avoids:

- Startup hype
- Generic sustainability language ("sustainable", "eco-friendly", "green")
- Over-spiritualised language in public copy
- Overclaiming ecological impact
- Reducing the work to "landscaping"
- Making the Registry sound like a directory
- Making Yield sound like a discount system

The Registry specifically runs a dual voice — Institutional (for councils, planners, partners: structured, conservative, time-stamped) and Steward (for homeowners: warm, plainspoken, legacy-minded). Both share one spine: integrity first, evidence before claim. See the Ecological Registry Brand Book for the full voice system.

### Words that matter

- **Steward, not owner.** A person is the current steward of a piece of land.
- **Indigenous, not native.** Indigenous is of this place, EVC-correct. Native is merely Australian.
- **Ecological, not sustainable.** Specific and measurable, not vague.
- **Function, not beauty,** when describing what the Registry measures.
- **Verified, not certified.** G&S verifies; it does not pretend to be an external standards body.
- **Network, not community,** for registered gardens — a measurable corridor, not a social promise.
- **Honest, not perfect.** A low score is a starting point, not a failure.

## 6. Visual system

The shared brand carries across all properties, with each property tuned to its role.

- **Gardener Green** `#3d4535` and **Nostalgic Beige** `#fff0dc` are the canonical G&S colours.
- **Abril Fatface** for large hero and wordmark moments — ceremonial, the family signature.
- **Fraunces** for editorial and institutional reading.
- **IBM Plex Sans** for body and interface; **IBM Plex Mono** for data, identity, and time-stamps.
- **Signal Green** is functional, not decorative — it appears only where meaning is being conveyed.
- **Border-radius zero.** No rounded corners anywhere. This is a registry and a record, not a consumer product.

The Ecological Registry Brand Book is the detailed standard for the Registry. Each property may tune the system to its purpose, but never contradicts the family.

## 7. The practical test

Before making any edit to any Gardener & Son property, ask:

1. Does this strengthen the whole system?
2. Does this still feel true to Gardener & Son?
3. Is it beautiful, useful, ecological, and legible?
4. Is the public language grounded?
5. Are the gardens, objects, registry, discovery, and incentives still connected?

If the answer to any of these is no, stop. Surface the conflict before proceeding. Do not resolve it silently.

## 8. What the Super Mind protects against

This document exists to hold the line against four specific failure modes as G&S grows and more hands — human and AI — touch the work:

- **Fragmentation.** Properties drifting into silos, each with its own logic, until the organism is just a portfolio.
- **Drift.** Tools and collaborators slowly eroding the tone, the design discipline, the scoring rigour.
- **Overclaiming.** Language running ahead of the honest sequence — implying Yield pays, implying certification, implying impact not yet measured.
- **Decoration.** Beauty becoming ornament; the Registry becoming a marketing scheme; meaning thinning into branding.

When in doubt, the Super Mind chooses coherence over cleverness, honesty over flattery, and the long term over the launch.
