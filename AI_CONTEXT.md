# AI_CONTEXT.md

Read this before editing any Gardener & Son web property.

For full context, read `SUPER_MIND.md`.

## Core Instruction

Do not treat Gardener & Son projects as separate websites.

They are expressions of one connected system:

Culture → Discovery → Registry → Incentives

Gardener & Son is building the private ecological infrastructure layer for cities.

## What This Means

Gardener & Son is not just a garden design studio.

It is:

- An ecological garden design studio
- An heirloom object house
- A public storytelling platform
- A registry for ecological gardens
- A discovery layer for place-based plants and EVCs
- A future incentives and aggregation system

## Tone

Use a voice that is:

- Calm
- Grounded
- Ecologically literate
- Beautiful but not decorative
- Intelligent but not corporate
- Human and humble
- Systems-aware

Avoid:

- Startup hype
- Generic sustainability language
- Over-spiritualised language in public copy
- Overclaiming ecological impact
- Reducing the work to "landscaping"
- Making the registry sound like a directory
- Making Yield sound like a discount system

## Canonical Colours

- Gardener Green: `#3d4535`
- Nostalgic Beige: `#fff0dc`

## Typography

- Abril Fatface for large hero moments
- IBM Plex Sans for body and interface
- Fraunces may be used for readable editorial serif moments

## The Minds

When making decisions, consider the relevant operating minds:

- Gardener Mind
- Designer Mind
- Ecologist Mind
- Systems Mind
- Steward Mind
- Civic Mind
- Commercial Mind
- Object Mind
- Storyteller Mind
- Super Mind

The Super Mind coordinates them all. Each is described in full in `SUPER_MIND.md`.

## Practical Rule

Before making edits, ask:

1. Does this strengthen the whole system?
2. Does this still feel true to Gardener & Son?
3. Is it beautiful, useful, ecological, and legible?
4. Is the public language grounded?
5. Are the gardens, objects, registry, discovery, and incentives still connected?

If the answer to any of these is no, stop and surface the conflict before proceeding.
